Bio

I'm Pamela Olowojebutu, a data engineer with a strong background in data science and machine learning. My passion lies in creating secure, efficient, and ethical data systems that empower organisations—especially in the healthcare and non-profit sectors—to make impactful decisions.

I have experience working across a wide range of technologies, including Apache Airflow, Azure, PostgreSQL, Salesforce, and AWS tools like Lambda and Bedrock. I currently support an international health NGO where I build data pipelines and contribute to meaningful solutions that improve access to healthcare for underserved populations.

I am also a Salesforce Associate-in-training and love learning how data, technology, and compassion intersect. My dream is to build products that enable positive change—especially for communities that often go unheard. One interesting fact about me? I once wrote a full pipeline while on a 10-hour road trip!

What I Do

Data Engineering

I build robust, scalable data pipelines using Apache Airflow, AWS Lambda, Databricks, and Azure Storage to automate extraction, transformation, and loading (ETL) processes. My pipelines support real-time and batch processing across APIs, cloud storage, and relational databases.

Salesforce & CRM Integration

I help organisations unlock the power of their CRM by connecting Salesforce data with external services and APIs. From custom data models to embedded analytics and automation flows, I support teams in better managing fundraising, health data, and client engagement.

AI-Driven Tools

I integrate machine learning and LLMs into tools that make data easier to access and interpret—such as recipe generators, healthcare record summaries, and smart dashboards. I use Python, AWS Bedrock, and secure API deployment to ensure practical and ethical outcomes.

Impact Projects

From building data pipelines for free surgical programs to contributing to UN-aligned SDG projects, I use tech for good. I manage data collection, compliance, analysis, and visualization to help NGOs measure impact, raise funds, and improve services.
